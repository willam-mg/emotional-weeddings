## Sophie Kaye Analysis

Observed patterns:

- Navigation emphasizes Portfolio, Experience, Blog, Inquire, and About.
- Hero positioning is immediate: editorial photographer, location, destination relevance.
- The page uses many images before asking users to read deeply.
- Press credibility appears early through "as seen in" style proof.
- Personal story is woven into brand value rather than hidden.
- Testimonials are emotional and specific.
- Destination positioning is supported by both imagery and copy.
- Portfolio categories help users self-select.

What to adapt:

- Stronger first-viewport photographer positioning.
- Portfolio-first journey.
- Press/publication credibility if authentic proof exists.
- Personal story as brand trust.
- Testimonial sections that talk about presence, calm, professionalism, and emotional memory.

What not to copy:

- NYC/Paris identity.
- Exact section sequence.
- Decorative historical/art references unless they fit Greg's brand.
- Copy style or testimonial wording.

## KT Merry Analysis

Observed patterns:

- Navigation is restrained and conversion-aware: Journal, Inquire, Offerings, About, Portfolio, Home.
- Positioning is global, luxury, destination, and editorial.
- The site uses large image sequences and editorial typography.
- Offerings are framed as a premium experience, not just packages.
- Portfolio categories include weddings, portraits, and editorial work.
- Press and authority are prominent.
- Copy uses cinematic phrases and high-level emotional promises before details.

What to adapt:

- "Offerings" mindset for Services.
- Strong inquiry pathway.
- Destination/global language where true.
- Editorial category pathways: Weddings, Engagements, Bridal Boudoir.
- Larger full-bleed or near-full-bleed image moments.
- Short premium copy blocks.

What not to copy:

- Celebrity/global scale unless Emotional Weddings can substantiate it.
- Exact typography pairings.
- Exact portfolio categories if they do not match available work.
- Publication claims without proof.

## Portfolio Patterns

Reference sites make portfolio browsing feel curated. Emotional Weddings should avoid a generic image dump and instead use:

- Featured editorial grid
- Category pathways: Weddings, Engagement, Bridal Boudoir
- Story-led gallery intros
- Fewer stronger images per section
- Consistent aspect ratios
- Clear "View full gallery" CTA

## Inquiry Patterns

Reference sites make inquiry feel like the natural next step after desire and trust. Emotional Weddings should:

- Place the first CTA after brand promise and selected portfolio proof.
- Use a strong CTA before footer on every page.
- Make Contact page feel personal, not just functional.
- Explain response expectation and next step.

## Typography And Spacing Observations

Both references lean on high-contrast editorial typography and generous spacing. Emotional Weddings should:

- Use a refined serif for large statements.
- Use a readable sans-serif for functional UI and body copy.
- Avoid overusing large all-caps.
- Keep body copy narrow.
- Use whitespace as a luxury signal.

## Image Sequencing Observations

Successful luxury photography sites sequence images like a story:

1. Immediate mood image.
2. Portrait/detail/couple variation.
3. Emotional proof.
4. Category pathway.
5. Testimonial or authority.
6. CTA.

Emotional Weddings should use image rhythm instead of stacking text-heavy explanations.

## Translation Into Emotional Weddings Principles

- Lead with "quiet, emotional, editorial documentary wedding photography."
- Make Greg's personality a trust asset.
- Let London/UK/destination positioning support search and aspiration.
- Move pricing details below emotional/value framing.
- Use fewer, stronger CTAs.
- Make every page feel like part of one journey.
